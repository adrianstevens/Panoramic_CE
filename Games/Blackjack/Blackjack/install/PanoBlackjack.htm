How To Play

The game of Blackjack

Panoramic Blackjack allows you to play one player Blackjack much like you would in many casinos. The goal of the game is to get a hand totaling as close to 21 as possible without "going over", as well as scoring more than the dealer. The specific rules of the game vary depending on which difficulty setting you have selected. Please see the Options section for more information.

The screen will show the dealer hand at the top, with the player hand below. The buttons to control gameplay appear at the bottom. You may press the Menu softkey at any time to exit.

To start a new game press "New Game" on the Menu screen. Before you begin play you must first place a bet. To place a bet, select or press one of the Bet buttons labeled $25, $50, $100, & $500. You may not bet more than you currently have in your bank. You can always add to your existing Bet, but must hit Reset to Bet a lower amount. To start a hand select the Deal button.

Once the cards have been dealt you will be able to hit, stand, double, surrender, split or buy insurance depending on the cards dealt and the skill setting chosen.

Controls

Blackjack may be played with or without a stylus (if your device supports it). To play with a stylus, simply tap the on screen buttons to activate.

To play without a stylus, you have two options depending on your device. To play with the 5-way control, press up/down/left/right to move the white onscreen selector. Once the selector is around the button you wish to activate, press the center of the 5-way control.

For devices with a numeric keypad (ie, 0-9 buttons), all of the onscreen buttons are mapped to the physical buttons. During the bet stage, the buttons will do the following:

1 - Deal
2 - Reset
3 - Bet $25
4 - Bet $50
5 - Bet $100
6 - Bet $500

When a hand is in play the buttons will do the following:

1 - Hit
2 - Stand
3 - Double (when available)
4 - Surrender (when available)
5 - Split (when available)
6 - Insurance (when available)

Card Counting - The Count

The idea behind counting cards in blackjack is that a deck of cards with a high proportion of high cards (ten-valued cards and aces) to low cards is good for the player, while the reverse (a deck with a high proportion of low cards to high cards) is good for the dealer. Basic card counting assigns a positive, negative, or zero value to each card. As each card is dealt, the running count ("The Count") is adjusted by each card's assigned value. A deck rich in tens and aces improves the player's odds because blackjacks (which offer a higher payout than other winning hands) become more common, the dealer is more likely to bust a stiff hand, and double-downs are more successful. *Please note - card counting may be considered cheating in some casinos.

Panoramic Blackjack saves your game state upon exit. To Resume your previous game simply choose Menu | Resume Game on application start up. Note: choosing New Game will erase your previous game and start a brand new game.

Options

To access the options, select the Options button from the Menu screen.

  • Play Sound: Toggles the sound on & off
  • Difficulty: Change the difficulty between Easy, Medium, & Difficult (see below), Learn to Play
  • Card Count: Toggles this feature on, on demand & off.
  • Card Count System: Choose between 7 different card counting systems, Wizard Ace/Five, KO, Hi-Lo, Hi-Opt I, Hi-Opt II, Zen Count, & Omega II.

The Difficulty Settings

Easy:

  • Game played with one (1) deck (auto shuffles)
  • Blackjack pays 2 to 1
  • Dealer hits on soft 17
  • Always shows the dealer's cards
  • Shows the current value of the player and dealer hands
  • Allows the option to surrender
  • Five (5) cards valued as 21

Normal:

  • Game played with two (2) decks (auto shuffles)
  • Blackjack pays 3 to 2
  • Dealer hits on soft 17
  • Allows the option to surrender

Difficult:

  • Game played with four (4) decks (auto shuffles)
  • Blackjack pays 3 to 2
  • Dealer stands on soft 17
  • Does not allow the option to surrender

Learn to Play:

  • Game played with one (1) deck (auto shuffles)
  • Blackjack pays 2 to 1
  • Dealer hits on soft 17
  • Always shows the dealer's cards
  • Allows the option to surrender
  • Five (5) cards valued as 21

Uninstalling

To uninstall from your Windows Mobile device:

  • Go to Start | Settings | System tab | Remove Programs.
  • Select on the name of the Panoramic game or application and tap "Remove".
  • Follow the onscreen instructions.

To uninstall from your Windows Mobile Smartphone:

  • Go to Start | Settings | Remove Programs.
  • Select on the name of the Panoramic game or application and tap "Remove".
  • Follow the onscreen instructions.

Your Panoramic Windows Mobile game or application should now have been uninstalled.

Note, when updating your Panoramic Software, you should uninstall any old versions before installing the new version. In most cases, you can install a new trial version to update an older full version unless the new version is a paid upgrade.
