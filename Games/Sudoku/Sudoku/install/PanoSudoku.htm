Overview

Panoramic Ultimate Sudoku features five challenging logic-based number puzzle games: Sudoku, Killer Sudoku, Kakuro, KenKen & Greater Than. In Sudoku the goal of the game is to fill the cells of a nine by nine grid with the digits 1 through 9. Kakuro combines the elements of Sudoku with the strategy of a crossword puzzle. Except the crossword clues are the total sum of all of the values in each vertical or horizontal section. Killer Sudoku combines elements of Sudoku with Kakuro - no initial numbers are given, but the nine by nine grid is divided into regions, each with a number that the sum of all numbers in the region must add up to and with no repeated numerals. These must be filled in while obeying the standard rules of Sudoku.

How To Play

Sudoku

Sudoku is a logical number placement puzzle game. The goal is to enter the numbers 1 through 9 in each cell of a 9 by 9 grid made up from nine 3 by 3 sub grids. The challenge is to place the numbers so each subgrid of 3 by 3 only has one instance of each number. As well, each vertical and each horizontal row of nine can only have one instance of each number.

Kakuro

Kakuro is crossword style number placement puzzle game. The goal is to enter a number from 1 through 9 in each empty cell of a grid, which is made up of a variable number of empty cells. The challenge is to place the numbers so each horizontal or vertical line only has up to one instance of any number with a total sum equal to the total values given to the left of the empty cells for the horizontal and above for the vertical.

Killer Sudoku

Killer Sudoku is a number placement puzzle game with attributes from both Sudoku and Kakuro. The goal is to enter the numbers 1 through 9 in each cell of a 9 by 9 grid made up from nine 3 by 3 sub grids. The challenge is to place the numbers so each subgrid of 3 by 3 only has one instance of each number. As well, each vertical and each horizontal row of nine can have only one instance of each number as well. With Killer Sudoku, the values within each outlined grouping must also add up to make the exact total given for each outline.

KenKen

Similar to Killer Sudoku, except all four mathematical operators (+-*/) are used.

Greater Than

Greater Than is a variant of Sudoku in which hints are provided by "greater than" symbols which show the relationship between adjacent numbers by indicating which number is greater. Each 3x3 subgrid must also contain each number from 1 to 9, and each row and column must contain one instance of each number from 1 to 9.

Controls

To begin play, simply tap on the game cell you wish to change. To insert a number then choose from the black numbered buttons. If you are unsure of your answer you may wish to insert a pencil mark and come back to this square later. To do this simply toggle between the "Pen and Pencil Icon" located on the Menu Bar. To solve the entire puzzle choose "Solve" located within the menu screen. To redo or choose another number, simply select the existing number and choose another number from the numbered buttons.

Menu Bar

Undo Icon: Undo your last move

Hint Icon: Randomly displays a number on the puzzle board

Pen & Pencil Icon: Toggle between Pen and Pencil. The "Pen Icon" allows you to insert a number on the board, while the "Pencil Icon" allows you to place small pencil marks within a square if you are unsure of the answer.

Menu Icon: Choose to start a "New Game", "Restart Game" or "Solve" the puzzle from within the Menu pop-up screen.

Options

To access the options, select the Options button from the Menu pop up.

Play Sounds: Enable or disable in game sounds.

Show Errors: Show errors when placing a guess - a red x will appear on incorrect cells.

Lock Completed: Shows a locked state when you complete a row, column or sub group. Not avaliable in Kakuro.

Show Hints: The number selection buttons will only highlight allowed guesses based on the board configuration.

Show Highlights: Highlights a entire row both horizontal and vertical when selecting a square. Choose between "Yes" or "No".

Draw blank cells (kakuro): Draws the non-playable cells on the game board in Kakuro. Choose between "Yes" or "No".

Skin: Choose between 10 different skins, "Glass", "Grey", "Wood", "Notepad", "Custom1", "Custom2", "Custom3", "Custom4", "Custom5", "Custom6", "Custom7", "Custom8", "Custom9", "Custom10".

Backgrounds: Choose between three (3) background choices: Camera, Image or Custom. Choosing "Camera" allows you to take a picture with your device camera and use it as the background image of your puzzle. Choosing "Image" allows you to browse your device for an image and use it as the background image of your puzzle. Choosing "Custom" will allow you to scroll through a list of preset colors or gradients and use them as the background to your puzzle.

Uninstalling

To uninstall from your Windows Mobile device:

  • Go to Start | Settings | System tab | Remove Programs.
  • Select on the name of the Panoramic game or application and tap "Remove".
  • Follow the onscreen instructions.

Your Panoramic Windows Mobile application should now have been uninstalled.
